Design work for ReView Technology Inc.

Introduction

This page houses my design work for ReView Technology Inc. ReView is a machine learning powered tool that allows users to know if any item is recyclable or compostable in their area with only a picture. Their goal is to create a greener future by making it easier to sort your disposables. More on them can be found here

Part 1: Logo Design

Task

Redesign the current logo to better represent the company.

Goals:

  • Create a combinational logo whose pictoral mark can be used stand alone as the app's icon
  • Use a set of colors that better represent the company's identity
  • Make the logo represent the app's functionality
  • Make the logo minimal and modern

Step 1: Sketches

This was my first time actually designing a Logo for a client. I began by sketching different concepts for the logo on my iPad using Procreate. The left side of the following image show some of the moteifs I was experimenting with including a camera, cycling arrows, the letter 'R', a magnifying glass (to represent searching), and some leaves (to represent the environment).

The right side of my divider shows 10 of the final logos I was most pleased with. The top rightmost a finished vector logo made in Adobe Illustrator, which I though would be a good final logo to present to the company. The finished logo is supposed to be a camera in the shape of a trashbin tying in the ideas of the app's core feature (using the camera) and what it detects (the user's disposables)

Feedback

When presenting my final logo to the company it was clear that they did not understand the concept of the logo. They could not tell that the body of the camera was supposed to represent a wastebin and though that the logo was lacking overall. They though that logos 6 and 7 were actually the best and encouraged me to try to move forward with those.

Iteration

I took the two favored logos and brought them into Illustrator and began to refine them.

However, after this design session, I wasn't particularly happy with any of the logos. Playing around different colors did not help improve the logos substantially either. I decided to reach out to another UX designer with more experience to help me. Together we sketched out some more and ended up refining the ideo of a leaf camera combining some of my previous sketches. I brought these ideas into illustrator and refined them into presentable logos to show my clients.

Finalizing the Logo

The company ended up really liking this new logo but had a similar issue to the first one: they could not tell that it was supposed to represent a leaf at first. To fix this, we experimented with more of a green color. I also though that the two tone wordmark would be good for separating the companies name but they were adamant about keeping it spelled "ReView"

After working with the founder, we ended up with a finalized logo with three primary colors that later became the basis of the company. We chose to stick with a simple font to capture the feeling of a technology startup. The logo met the goals of being a pictoral or combinational logo, used greens and blues to represent recycling, and symbolized the company's goal of creating a greener future, all while being modern and relatively minimal.
